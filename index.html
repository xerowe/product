Alopecia (baldness, from Latin. alopecia - baldness, hair loss) - abnormal hair loss which leads to thinning or complete loss in certain head, face or torso areas. The most common types of alopecia - androgenic, diffusive or symptomatic, telogen effluvium, alopecia areata, dermatophytosis, scar alopecia.

Causes of alopecia are genetic – it’s mainly the fault of harmful effect on the hair follicles of an active form of the male sex hormone testosterone — dihydrotestosterone, which is produced under the influence of the 5-alpha-reductase enzyme, contained in hair follicles. The penetration of dihydrotestosterone into the follicle cells causes their degeneration and, finally, hair dystrophy.
